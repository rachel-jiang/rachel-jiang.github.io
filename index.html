Domestic Cat Breeds

Cat is a small domestic species of small carnivorous mammal. It is the only domestic animal in its family Falidae. A cat can be house cat, farm cat or feral cat. There are about 60 cat breeds recognized by various cat registries. Cats have strong and flexible bodies, sharp teeth, retractable claws and quick reflexes, they are adapted to kill small prey. Female domestic cats usually have kittens between spring to late autumn and they usually have 2 to 5 kittens.

Bengal Cats

Bengal cat is a domestic species created from hybrids of domestic cats (for example spotted Egyptian Mau) and Asian leopard cat. The name of this breed comes from the leopard cat's taxonomic name. Bengal cats have spots, rosettes, arrowhead markings, or marbling on their coat. They are energetic and needs a lot of exercise and play. reference

Bengal cats are usually affectionate with family, playful, intelligent and kid and pet friendly. They usually live around 10 to 16 years and are 17 to 22 inches long not including their tail. They weight around 8 to 17 pounds and orignicated from the USA. reference

American shorthair

American Shorthair is a breed of domestic cat. It is believed that this cat breed is a descendant of European cats and was brought to North America by the early settlers. It was the 7th most popular \pedigreed cat in the US in 2012 according to the Cat Fanciers' Association. They have a powerful body despite not being an athletic cat and is a true breed of working cat according to the Cat Fanciers' Association. They usually have round faces and short ears. reference

American shothair are usually friendly with strangers and kids and are very intelligent. They have a life span for around 15 to 20 years. They are adaptable and retained their hunting abilities. reference

Ragdoll

Ragdoll cats are affectionate with family, pet friendly and kid friendly. They usually live between 12 to 17 years and are usually 17 to 20 inches long no including their tail. They weight around 10 to 20 pounds and originated from California, USA. Ragdolls are easy to live with, have nice manner and can learn quickly. reference

Ragdolls loves people and can even get along with d ogs. They are big cats and mgiht need to be supported by 2 hands when held. The breed was created by mixing 3 cats with an unknown heritage in the 1960s. They come in six colors (seal, blue, chocolate, lilac, red and cream) and four patterns (mitted, van, bicolor and colorpoint). They mature relatively slowly and reach their full size when they are around 4 years old. reference

British Shorthair

British shorthair is pet friendly, kid friendly and friendly toward strangers. They are not very playful and are generally healthy. They can live around 12 to 17 years and are approximately 22 to 25 inches long not including the tail. They usually weight between 7 to 17 pounds and originated form the Great Britain. reference

Blue is the most popular color of British Shorthair, other color and patterns includes white, black, cream, various tabby patterns, bi-colors etc. Their eye color varies depending on what color or pattern their coat is. reference

Scottish Fold

The Scottish Fold is a cat breed that has a natural dominant gene that cause their ears to fold by bending forward and down towards their head. They were originally called lop-ear or lops, named after the lop-ear rabbit. At different registries, longhaired Scottish Fold has different names such as Highland Fold, Scottish Fold Longhair, Longhair fold. reference

Scottish folds are usually kid and pet friendly and are very intelligent.They live around 11 to 14 years and are about 10 to 12 inches long excluding their tail. They weight around 6 to 13 pounds and originated form Tayside, Scotland. reference

Cheetoh

Cheetoh Cat, a feline with similar looks of a wildcat combined with gentle temperament of domesticated shorthat, are good pets. Cheetoh cats are intelligent, affectionate, and playful.

Carol Drymon (from Wind Haven Exotics Cattery) bred the first Cheetoh Cat in 2003. The purpose was to create the most wild-looking domesticated cat ever without having to introduce new wild blood. Cheetoh was bred from an Ocicat and a Bengal. reference

Egyptian Mau

Egyptian Maus, small to medium-sized short hair cat breed, are one of the few naturally spotted breeds of domesticated cats. They are considered a rare breed. Egyptian Maus are a relatively rare breed with fewer than 200 kittens are registered with the Governing Council of the Cat Fancy each year although not all Mau kittens are registered. reference

Savannah Cat

Savannah cat is a breed born from cross between serval and a domesticated cat. They are tall and slim, having a bigger appearance than their actual weight. Their size however is dependent on their generation and gender. F1 hybrid male cats are usually the largest. F1 and F2 generations are usually the largest, as a result of serval's genetic influence. Similar to other hybrid cats, most of the first-generation cats will possess many of serval's exotic traits which would diminish in later generations. Males tend to be largar than females. reference

African Wildcat

African Wildcat (Felis lybica), a small wildcat native to Africa, West and Central Asia to Rajasthan (India) and Xinjiang (China), were first domesticated around 10, 000 years ago in Near East. They are considered the ancestors of domestic cat (Felis Catus). Crossings between African Wildcats and domestic cats are common today. reference

Serval

Serval (Leptailurus serval), a wild cat native to Africa, is rare in North Africa and Sahel and common in sub-Seharan countries (except rainforest regions). Hunting them is prohibited or regulated in countries in this range. Serval is a medium-sized slender cat. They stand around 54-62 cm at the shoulder and weighs 9=18 kg. They have small heads, large ears, light yellow fur coat spotted and striped with black, and a short tail with black tip. Serval has the longest legs of any cats (comparing to their body size). reference

Lion

Lion (Panthera leo) powerful cat (family Felidae) is smaller in size only when compared to the tiger. They are mostly active at night and live in a variety of habitats. Lions perfer grassland, savanna, dense scrub, and open woodland. They ranged across much of Europe, Asia, and Africa historically but now are found mainly in parts of Africa south of the Sahara. reference

Lynx

Lynx (genus Lynx) consists of four species of short tail cats (family Felidae). They are mostly found in the forests of Europe, Asia, and North America. Canada lynx (Lynx canadensis) and bobcat (Lynx rufus) live in North America; Eurasian lynx (Lynx lynx) and Iberian lynx (Lynx pardinus) live in Europe. Iberian lynx is the most endangered. Lynx have long legs and large paws. They have tufted ears, hairy soles, and a short, wide head. reference

Leopard

Leopard (Panthera pardus), one of the five extant species in the genus Panthera, is a memeber of Felidae and appears in a wide range in sub-Saharan Africa, small parts of Western and Central Asia, a small range of European Russai, and the Indian subcontinent to Southeast and East Asia. They are listed Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List as their populationas are threatened by habitat loss and fragmentation. They are declining in large parts in the global range. reference

Tiger

Tiger (Panthera tigris), the largest livign cat species, a member of genus Panthera, is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on dark orange fur with a lighter underside. Tigers are apex predator and primarily eats ungulates like deers and wild boar. Their are territorial and generally solitary but social predators. They require large contiguous habitats that support its requirements for prey and rearing of offsprings. Tiger cubs stay with their mother for around two years and then leave their mother's home range to establish their own territory. reference

Cheetah

Cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus), a large cat native to Africa and central Iran, is the fastest land animal (estimated able to run 80-128 km/h). They have adaptations for speed, for instance, a lightly built, long, thin, legs and a long tail. They usually reach 67-94 cm at the shoulder, and the head-and-body length is approximarely 1.1-1.5m. Adult cheetahs weight around 20-65 kg. A cheetah has a small, round head, a short snout, and black tear-like facial stipes. Their coat is typically tawny to creamy white or pale buff and is mostly covered with evenly spaced, solid black dots. reference

Jaguar

Jaguar (Panthera onca), a large felid species and the only living member of genus Panthera native to America, has distinctively marked coat with pale yellow to tan colored fur and covered by spots that transits to darker rosettes on the sides. They have body lengths up to 1.85 m and is the largest cat species in the Americas and the third largest in the world. They have powerful bites which is capable of piercing the carapaces of turtles and can bite directly through the skull of prey between ears to deliver a fatal blow to the brain. reference

Black Panther

Black Panther is a color variant of the leopard (Panthera pardus) and the jaguar (Panther onca). Black panthers of both species have excess black pigments while their spotted markings are present as well. reference
